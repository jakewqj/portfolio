## The brief

Donner asked me to lead PR for the Triple Threat launch in late 2023. Going in, the brand had a real positioning problem: most reviewers who covered our hardware led with the price comparison and treated the pedal as a value pick rather than a serious tool. That ceiling was hard to break through with another in-house product release. NAMM 2024 was the window to try something different, and the Triple Threat, a three-channel multi-effects pedal, was what Donner picked to do it with.

The mandate I worked to: get Triple Threat reviewed and held up against named-brand pedals in the same paragraph, by publications whose editors don't normally cover us at all.

## Why Third Man

The collaboration with Jack White's Third Man Records was the move. It changed what the pedal was, journalistically. A spec sheet from Donner is a routine New Gear note. A Donner pedal co-designed by Third Man is a story about Jack White's gear philosophy, about an independent label spec-ing hardware, about an emerging brand earning that partnership. Three angles, three different desks.

I treated the Third Man relationship as the press hook from the first pitch onward, not the product specs. The specs were the proof. The lede was always the partnership.

## The angle I pitched

I split the rollout into three story lines and pitched each to a different kind of writer:

- **Reviews desk** — pedal head-to-head against the multi-effects category leaders. Premier Guitar, Guitar Player, Total Guitar, MusicRadar, Sound on Sound.
- **News desk** — the announcement itself, framed as Donner's first artist-label co-brand. Guitar World, Guitar.com, Music Inc, Music Connection, plus mainland coverage like 新华网 and Midifan.
- **Trade / industry** — the business angle. Music Inc and MMR cared about what the deal signaled for value-tier brands moving into named partnerships.

Keeping the three tracks distinct meant I wasn't sending the same pitch to dozens of inboxes hoping one stuck. Each editor got a version of the story they could actually run.

## Execution timeline

I built the campaign around four waves, each with its own job.

**Pre-NAMM seed (early January 2024).** Four embargoed pieces with the outlets I most needed to anchor the launch: Premier Guitar, Guitar.com, Guitar World, and a Reverb listing. The goal was to lock in the framing before the show floor opened.

**NAMM launch (late January through mid-February 2024).** The bulk of the wave. I coordinated press visits at Donner's booth with Third Man's team on hand, ran on-site demos, and timed the review-unit drops so the first wave of pieces landed inside the NAMM news cycle and continued through the post-show weeks. This is where Music Inc handed us the NAMM 2024 Editor's Choice on the floor.

**Q2 review wave (May 2024).** By then the units had been on reviewers' boards long enough for considered write-ups. This is where the print awards landed: Total Guitar named it Best Buy in their August 2024 issue (#386), Guitar Player gave it the Editor's Pick in the May issue.

**Best-of-year cycle and long tail (Q4 2024 onward).** Guitar World folded Triple Threat into Best Gear of 2024. MusicRadar and Guitar World both came back for revisit pieces in mid-2025. By 2026 the tail was still moving — Bass Magazine ran a fresh piece in April 2026, more than two years after launch.

## Results

Four named editorial awards. Total Guitar Best Buy, Guitar Player Editor's Pick, Music Inc NAMM 2024 Editor's Choice, and Guitar World Best Gear of 2024. The named recognition was the part that mattered most to me. Best Buy and Editor's Pick are bylines editors stake their own taste on. Music Inc's NAMM Editor's Choice is decided by editors walking the floor. Guitar World putting Triple Threat into Best Gear of 2024 closed the loop — same magazine that ran the launch news, choosing the product again months later on the merits.

Geographic spread was wider than I'd expected going in. US Tier 1 was the priority, but the campaign also picked up UK (Total Guitar, Guitar Player, Guitar.com, Guitar Interactive), Canada (Exclaim), Australia, and mainland China (新华网, Midifan, EXOUND). For a product launched at a US trade show, that's a healthier distribution than I would have predicted.

The long tail surprised me too. Most pedal launches go dark after the second review wave. Triple Threat kept getting written about into 2025 and 2026, which I read as evidence that the Third Man association gave editors a reason to come back to the product on a slow news week.

## What I'd do differently, and what carried over

Two things I'd change. First, the pre-NAMM seed list was too narrow. I should have included Sound on Sound and at least one European print magazine in the embargo group, instead of waiting for them to pick it up post-launch. Second, I didn't push hard enough on video. The YouTube reviews we got were good but trailed the print cycle by weeks. On the next campaign I built video assets into the embargo window from the start.

What carried forward: the three-track pitching framework (reviews / news / trade) became the default for every artist-partnership launch after this. HUSH-X in 2024 and the L1 synth launch in 2025 both used the same structure. The Triple Threat result also gave me the internal credibility to argue for bigger artist partnerships at NAMM 2026.
